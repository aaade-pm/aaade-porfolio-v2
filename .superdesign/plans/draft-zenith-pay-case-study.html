Zenith Pay

Live Website

Role

Lead Frontend Engineer

Context

Fintech Dashboard

Timeline

4 Months (Q1 2024)

Stack

React, Tailwind, Framer

The Challenge: Simplifying Global Wealth

Overview

Zenith Pay is a high-fidelity digital banking ecosystem designed for high-net-worth individuals who require extreme visual clarity for complex data. The project involved reimagining the entire transaction lifecycle—from initiation to deep analytical reporting—into a single, cohesive interface.

The Problem

Most existing fintech dashboards suffer from "data fatigue." Users were overwhelmed by cluttered spreadsheets, inconsistent charting libraries, and slow load times for real-time transaction streams. Zenith Pay needed to feel like a high-end luxury watch: precise, elegant, and instantly readable.

The Approach

01.

Architecture

Establishing a robust design system and component library in React to ensure visual consistency across 40+ unique screens and 120+ micro-states.

02.

Motion Design

Implementing Framer Motion to create spatial awareness. Every transaction card slide and chart transition was choreographed to feel fluid and natural.

03.

Optimization

Refining the data-fetching layer with SWR and custom caching strategies to handle thousands of real-time records without dropping a single frame.

Visual Narrative

A focus on deep charcoal surfaces, high-contrast typography, and purposeful use of our signature olive accent.

The Outcome

98%

Performance Score

Average Lighthouse score across all dashboard pages after intensive code splitting.

45%

Faster Onboarding

Reduction in average time-to-first-transaction through simplified UX flow.

1.2s

Data Refresh Rate

Synchronous real-time updates for global market transaction streams.

Lessons Learned

"Complexity is a design challenge, not just a technical one. Building Zenith Pay taught me that the best way to handle 'too much data' is to design for hierarchy first. If everything is important, nothing is."

"We also discovered that motion is a functional tool. Adding 200ms transitions between states didn't just look good—it helped users mentally track where their context was moving within the app."

Olatunji Ademola

Lead Engineer
